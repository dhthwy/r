Engines, transmissions may be good these days, but they encase it with cheap plastic some of which is critical.

Especially on an interference engine where if anything fails, goodbye motor. Timing chains aren't immune when they depend on plastic parts to keep in time.

The only thing good about my car is the steering components (same as used on mustangs), suspension, engine, and transmission which are the most important parts.

But all the rest is garbage.

They use cheap ABS plastic for the thermostat housing (a downgrade from earlier cars which used the more durable glass-reinforced nylon). Rewind back further in time they used all metal housings. Last forever.

So they spring a catastrophic leak every 50k more or less miles, causing an overheat, and probably the #1 culprit these cars end up in the junkyard.

Only need to overheat a vehicle once to blow the head gasket and warp the aluminum block. If you're not reading the coolant temp gauge to catch it before it hits the red line, boom.

That's how my last car died. Coolant leak, overheated. "Home was only 2 miles away, they say." Yeah, that's all it takes. That's why I don't like anyone else driving my car.

Car prices are so ridiculous, and people thinking they can keep their plastic cars long time to justify the cost are in for a rude awakening.
